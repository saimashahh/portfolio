My Story

Hi there,

I'm based in Alberta, Canada. With a background in Psychology and Information Systems and a passion for continuous learning, I am transitioning to UX Design. I appreciate the importance of usabilty and how frustration creates cognitive overload, especially because of my background as a Counselling Therapist. I find I apply a lot of what I learned as a Therapist to designing products.

Earlier in my work history, I learned how to cope with ambiguity and the importance of usability first hand. while working at a payments startup.

Even though my UX Design journey officially started in 2023, it initiated much earlier in the role of content writer at the above mentioned company, since I conducted product research, competitive analysis, usability tests and designed engaging user guides and help documentation. In my UX Design journey, I have developed design thinking skills coupled with Figma skills to be able to translate a design idea into wireframes for rapid prototyping.

I also use Microsoft and Google suite of products, Adobe Creative Suite and Canva. I am nearly always thinking of a way to connect to the user's pain point. My design process is that after researching the project, I close all external distractions and draw on an iPad or pencil and paper on dotted grid. I find that putting in the effort to design from scratch on a blank canvas creates a stronger empathetic connection with the user's journey and the end design is simpler, with fewer fewer assumptions about the user's journey.

As a designer with a Frontend development focus, I can communicate with frontend developers and design with development in mind.

Outside of Counselling Therapy and UX design, I enjoy hiking and gardening in rural Alberta.

I'm excited about knowing more about your project, contact me and let's talk about it.

Yours truly, Saima
