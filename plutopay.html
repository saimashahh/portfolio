PlutoPay Case Study

Welcome to the UX Design Portfolio for the PlutoPay Project

The Problem

Competitive analysis and secondary research showed that even though the financial app market was quite saturated with fintech apps, there was limited understanding regarding user lifestyles and needs of a large user niche: 'Digital nomads' in the age range of 19-40, who travel the world for work and study.

  • Digital nomads need to exchange currency quickly and efficiently.
  • They need to make payments in multiple currencies.
  • Budget and financial management is challenging because of exchange rate variability, wi-fi issues, travel, language, security, and differences in time zones.

Users need an efficient and secure online payment product that they can use to shop, travel, send money, and manage their budget easily and intuitively.

The Process

The process for developing the product started with user research and persona development. Relevant user stories guided user flows and the site map. This was tested with users in the demographic and psychographic range relevant to the problem. Testing methods included card sorting, user interviews, affinity mapping, and preference testing. During the iterative design process, wireframes evolved from low-fidelity to high-fidelity designs.
