PlutoPay Case Study

PlutoPay was designed using design thinking and user centric design to create a solution to the user's problem.

The Problem

Competitive analysis and secondary research showed that even though the financial app market was quite saturated with Fintech app, there was limited understanding regarding user lifestyles and needs of a large user niche; 'Digital nomads' in the age ranges of 19-40, who travel the world for work and study. Three main themes emerged. 1. Digital nomads need to exchange currency quickly and efficiently. 2. They need to make payments in multiple currencies. 3. Budget and financial management is challenging because of exchange rate variability, wi-fi issues, travel, language, security and differences in time zones

Users need an efficient and secure online payment product which they can use to shop, travel or send money easily and manage their budget from anywhere, easily and intuitively.

The Process

The process for developing the product started with user reearch and persona development. Relevant user stories guided user flows and site map. All of this was tested with users in the demographic and psychographic range relevant to the problem. Testing methods included card sorting, user interviews, affinity map and preference testing. Usability Tests

Lofi Prototype Using a Lofi prototype usability tests were conducted.

In this phase the goal was to: Identify usability issues and severity Understand user perspective Improve user experience Discoverability of features. We conducted a combination of in person and remote tests with 6 participants who reflected the persona of the app and learned about several usability issues ranging from mild to severe. Severe usability issues were immediately resolved through the next phase of the design and mockups were created that reflected what we learned from testing.
